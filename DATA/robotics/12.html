About the Job

Position Responsibilities:

  • Client specification review for understanding and development of exceptions if required.
  • Timely development of controls estimates for system quotations for both electrical and pneumatic controls systems.
  • Controls design including but not limited to process flow diagrams, panel layout, termination diagrams and field wiring pull schedules are required accordance with NEC, NFPA and UL specifications.
  • Component specification, development of bills or materials, solicitation of vendor quotations and, with the assistance of purchasing, ordering of components in accordance with manufacturing requirements.
  • Either directly or with support of the controls group, develop any required PLC and HMI programs.
  • Scheduling of work through the electrical shop
  • Open communications including status updates with the rest of the KleenLine engineering and management teams.
  • Client communications as required to clarify questions, specification concerns or installation issues.
  • Take part in necessary project and product meetings
  • Assist in startup and debug as required either at KleenLine or at the client facility

Education and Experience:

  • Bachelors of Engineering Degree or equivalent from a four-year college or equivalent industry experience.
  • 5 - 7 years experience designing and implementing process control solutions, programming PLCs, and implementing HMIs.  Demonstrated experience with PLC controlled automated equipment with Siemens and/or Rockwell PLC language required.

Specific Knowledge, Skills, Licenses, Certifications Etc:

  • Design experience of industrial electrical controls systems required.
  • Demonstrated programming experience with Siemens and/or Allen Bradley PLC/HMI hardware and software.  Working knowledge of Rockwell Software suite of programming tools (Logix 5000, RSView ME, and PanelBuilder)
  • Demonstrated experience conceiving, designing and implementing process controls on high speed machinery including closed loop controls, robots, rotary and linear indexing mechanisms with servo and VFD motors and drives, pneumatic and electric actuators and controls, and sensors.
  • Mechanical and electrical integration experience required.
  • Desired process knowledge of: high-speed assembly, printing, packaging, robotics, materials handling systems and utility systems.
  • Demonstrated electrical design skills using AutoCAD.
  • Capable with computer software applications including MS Office and  MS Project
  • Must possess excellent interpersonal, communication, documentation, presentation and computer skills.  Works in a team based environment and exchanges technical information with management, technical support personnel, and equipment vendors.
  • Must be self directed and resourceful, detail oriented and able to manage multiple priorities.  Must be flexible to accept assignments in support of overall site goals.
  • Must be able to influence individuals with and without supervisory authority and motivate others within an extended project team that includes outside contractors and vendors.
  • Ability to read, analyze, and interpret the most complex documents.
  • Ability to apply principles of logical or scientific thinking to a wide range of intellectual and practical problems.
  • Ability to deal with a variety of abstract and concrete variables
  • Knowledge of general material handling systems and specific knowledge of food and pharmaceutical requirements.
  • Ability to manage multiple priorities
  • Ability to write presentations and reports using original or innovative techniques or style.
  • Ability to make effective and persuasive arguments and presentations on controversial or complex topics to top management, public groups, and/or boards of directors.
  • Willingness to apply additional effort and time as required by a project or deadline.
