About the Job


About the Job

Reporting to the Team Lead, the.Net Developer is responsible for design, programming, developing, documenting and maintaining web-based applications for the central Department of Education (DOE) websites utilizing the Microsoft.Net framework in a Microsoft Windows environment. 


Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Creates mission critical distributed system design solutions which include data modeling, object modeling, and proposes screen designs by gathering requirements from end users, management and other stakeholders.
  • Utilizes Object Oriented design patterns to coordinate the development and creation of projects and project plans and architectural software solutions, and establishes best practice solutions for architectural design.
  • Develops N-Tier web applications utilizing design patterns based on ASP.NET web form technology, and designs .NETuser and custom controls integrated with web form (code behind) for User Interface (UI) presentation.
  • Ensures compliance with documented software processes and procedures throughout the life cycle of the software product.
  • Designs, develops and implements critical applications in a .Net environment.  This includes implementing best practices, standards and procedures.
  • Installs and implements relational database solutions utilizing SQL Server 2005 and T-SQL.
  • Collaborates with graphic designers in the use of advanced Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) in coordination with other graphic designers to add style to web documents in development.
  • Develops user training documentation in accordance with Department of Education standards and trains end users.


Please Note:   Applicant must be a US Citizen or Possess a Green Card to be eligible for this position


Qualifications
Minimum Requirements

  1. A baccalaureate degree from an accredited college, including or supplemented by 24 semester credits in computer science or a related computer field and two years of satisfactory full-time computer software experience in computer systems development and analysis,applications programming, database administration, systems programming, data communications, including one year in a project leader capacity or as a major contributor on a complex project; or
  2. A four-year high school diploma or its educational equivalent and six years of satisfactory full-time computer software experience as described in "1" above, including one year in a project leader capacity or as a major contributor on a complex project; or
  3. Education and/or experience equivalent to "1" or "2" above. College education may be substituted for up to two years of the required experience in "2" above on the basis that 60 semester credits from an accredited college is equated to one year of experience. A masters degree in computer science or a related computer field may be substituted for one year of the required experience in "1" or "2" above. However, all candidates must have at least a four-year high school diploma or its educational equivalent and at least one year of satisfactory full-time experience in a project leader capacity or as a major contributor on a complex project.


Preferred:

  • A minimum of 5 years of experience working  independently to code, optimize and test ASP.net controls using, C#, JavaScript, and JQuery should have experience in all phases of SDLC.
  • Good writing and verbal communication skills.

  • Experience in database design is preferred.
  • System Analysis Skills desired.
  • Experience in writing technical documentation is preferred.
  • Working knowledge of Internet/Intranet Application Services.

  • Strong knowledge & working experience with ASP.NET 2.0/3.0/3,5/4.0.
  • Experience with working within a Content Management System/SharePoint.
  • Strong JavaScript, CSS, and HTML skills.
  • Experienced in Agile Development techniques eg: Test Driven Design, Extreme Programming, SCRUM, etc.
