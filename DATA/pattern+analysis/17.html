Practice Manager Consultant - Clinically Integrated Physician Organization - MHMD

BASIC FUNCTION:
Position is responsible for the establishment and ongoing management of relationships with Memorial Hermann Physician Network (MHMD) physicians and their office staff regarding Memorial Hermann resources such as Physician incentive plans, technology, and external resources deployed to advance Patient Centered Medical Home (PCMH) and Clinical Integration initiatives. This includes serving as the primary liaison with MHMD physicians to coordinate the implementation, training, support, and ongoing maintenance of all such resources as well as the tracking and reporting of usage of these resources. This position is also responsible for providing support and assistance to MHMD and MHMD physicians to achieve targets set forth in established physician incentive programs and to receive recognition and/or designation from national accrediting bodies (NCQA, Bridges to Excellence, etc.), insurance companies or other respective entities for accomplishments in disease management, population management, medical cost management and adoption of PCMH principles. This will involve coordinating and monitoring application processes, tracking and reporting adherence to established benchmarks and observing and making process redesign recommendations to achieve higher compliance and performance. This role will perform these functions in a territory to include up to 25 MHMD practices.

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:
1. Bachelor's degree in related field OR the equivalent combination of education and experience necessary to effectively perform job functions
2. At least two years of healthcare experience in an outpatient physician practice setting with an understanding of physician needs, practice patterns and workflow.
3. Strong communication and presentation skills required.
4. Interpersonal skills necessary in order to interact effectively and build strong relationships with physicians, staff and all levels of management.
5. Excellent organizational and prioritization skills; ability to lead multiple projects at the same time and obtain desired outcomes on schedule.
6. Analytical skills necessary to identify customer needs in order to develop action plans.
7. Working knowledge of Microsoft Office - Excel, Word, and Power Point.
8. Working knowledge of major quality improvement efforts (PQRI, NCQA, CI, HEDIS, etc.).
9. Ability to drive to MHMD meetings and MHMD physician offices in regional area.

PRINCIPAL ACCOUNTABILITIES:
1. Serves as primary point of contact between MHMD and MHMD physicians and their office staff regarding the implementation and usage of Memorial Hermann resources deployed to advance PCMH and Clinical Integration initiatives.
2. Establishes and manages MHMD physician and physician office relationships.
3. Assists and coordinates the implementation, training, support, and ongoing maintenance of technology including Crimson Care Registry to MHMD physician practices and tracks and reports utilization of these resources. Troubleshoots and course corrects as necessary.
4. Manages application processes for MHMD and MHMD physician practices to receive recognition and/or designation from national accrediting bodies (NCQA, Bridges to Excellence, etc.), insurance companies or other respective entities for accomplishments in disease management, population management, medical cost management and adoption of PCMH principles.
5. Trains, supports and assists MHMD and MHMD physicians to achieve targets and benchmarks set forth in established physician incentive programs.
6. Provides regular, consistent feedback to MHMD and MHMD physicians on measurable results. Assists physicians in development of action plans to improve results.
7. Maintains the requisite skills to complete analysis on data and processes in order to recognize and analyze undesirable patterns or trends in usage or performance and obtains the needed resources to develop strategies for redesign and intervention to demonstrate improvement.
8. Monitors and communicates trends and issues that may affect MHMD physician relationships.
9. Travels throughout regional area to meet with MHMD physicians and office staff; Attends MHMD and MHMD physician office meetings and conducts presentations.
10. Analyzes and provides hands-on support for moderate to complex inquiries. Determines appropriate technical area or vendor to resolve a reported issue and coordinates with other respective areas, as needed to achieve resolution.
11. Recommends process changes that improve the implementation, maintenance and support of hardware, software, and/or services deployed by MHMD.
12. Works with the vendors and appropriate MHMD staff and departments to develop standardized policies and procedures and deploy best practices in the utilization of deployed MHMD resources.
13. Demonstrates the ability to lead or influence others positively to achieve desired work results.
