About the Job

Raytheon's Electrical Design Directorate (EDD) provides engineering design, integration and production to core radar, sonar, torpedo and combat system competencies including process and control, integration and test, power systems, torpedo and sonar electronics, receivers and exciters, antennas and microwave subassemblies.


Job Description


 


As a semiconductor device modeling engineer within the Foundry Services Group you will be responsible for supporting Raytheon design engineers by characterizing active and passive devices, and creating device models and extracting model parameters for the EDA design tools in use by the various design teams across Raytheon. The ideal candidate will be able to support a wide range of semiconductor device technologies and EDA flows with primary support for the characterization and modeling of devices from the Raytheon Integrated Defense Systems foundry in Andover, MA. A solid understanding of semiconductor device physics and device characterization techniques as well excellent communication skills are a must.


 


Responsibilities will include some or all of the following:


 



  • Apply advanced technical principles, theories and concepts to provide innovative solutions to highly complex technical problems
  • Develop advanced technological ideas and guide their development into a final product
  • Design and layout of device modeling test structures and chips
  • Evaluation, use, and support of measurement and parameter extraction hardware and software
  • Execution of device characterization test plans including static & pulsed I-V, C-V, CW & pulsed bias S-Parameter, load/source-pull, TOI, and noise measurements
  • Analyzing measured device data and extracting device models
  • Developing advanced device model formulations and model extraction algorithms
  • Publishing device model documentation and foundry design manuals
  • Assisting device and process engineers in analyzing and understanding the detailed performance of various semiconductor active and passive devices through direct measurement and/or TCAD simulation
  • Assisting EDA engineers in developing and testing MMIC Process Design Kits (PDKs) in Agilent Advanced Design System (ADS) and AWR Microwave Office as well as Analog/Mixed-Signal (AMS) PDKs in Cadence Virtuoso 

    This position can be a E05 or E06 based on the candidate's qualifications as they relate to the skills, experience and responsibility requirements for the position.

Skills & Experience
