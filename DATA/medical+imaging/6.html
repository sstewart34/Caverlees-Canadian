Radiologic Technologist

POSITION SUMMARY:

The Radiologic Technologist - Non-Registered is responsible for applying ionizing radiation to demonstrate portions of the human body on a radiographic, fluoroscopic screen, or other imaging modality to assist the physician in the diagnosis of disease or injury. This is a non-registered position and is used pending registration. The incumbent works under the general guidance, supervision and direction of the Medical Imaging Manager. Additionally, the incumbent works under the daily operational oversight of the Radiologic Technologist Team Leader.

The incumbent knows and abides by all Group Health Cooperative of South Central Wisconsin (GHC-SCW) organizational and departmental policies, sets personal standards and strives for high quality work in completing assignments, performs job duties in a timely manner, and represents the organization in a positive manner. The incumbent understands our Mission, Vision and Common Values (stated below) and is committed to promote these values in behavior and attitude.

JOB SPECIFIC QUALIFICATIONS

1.Graduation from an accredited Radiology technology program or current senior status in a Radiology technology program is required.
2.Registry eligible Radiologic Technologist by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT). As a condition of employment, incumbent must obtain ARRT registration within one (1) year.
3.One (1) year of radiologic experience is preferred.
4.Possession of or eligibility for a valid Healthcare Provider CPR/AED certificate is required. Employee must obtain certification within (4) months of employment.
5.Maintenance of current continuing medical education credits is required.
6.Knowledge of general and breast anatomy is required.
7.Knowledge of the physical and operational characteristics of radiographic and /or mammography equipment is required.
8.Knowledge of the principles and practices utilized in radiographic and/or mammographic examination, and knowledge of radiation exposure techniques is required.
9.Ability to recognize normal and abnormal structures as depicted on ultrasound in order to tailor the exam to answer the clinical question is required.
10.Knowledge of and ability to adhere to OSHA standards and other patient care protocols is required.
11.Good communication and interpersonal skills are required.
12.Ability to maintain patient confidentiality required. Knowledge of or ability to learn HIPAA privacy requirements.
13.Ability to work with a diverse patient population is required.
14.Excellent customer service skills are required.
15.Ability to stand for up to 90% of a workday is required.
16.Ability to lift from waist level and carry loads ranging from 15-50 lbs. is required.
17.Ability to push and pull radiographic equipment is required.
18.Ability to handle, finger, control left or right hand and reach (above shoulder, over 18" and to the floor) is required.
19.Near and midrange vision is required.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

1.Performance of routine radiographic studies as ordered by physicians. [84%]

a.Determine, prepare and set up medical supplies, instruments and equipment.
b.Communicate procedure instructions and education to patients and physicians.
c.Prepare patient for diagnostic procedures to include taking vital signs and measurements and properly position the patient, film and markers for optimal radiographic results for each anatomical view.
d.Ally patient fears and elicit cooperation of patient.
e.Perform a variety of technical tasks involved in operating x ray equipment to make radiographs of designated positions of the body as specified by the physician.
f.Adjust equipment for proper exposure, voltage, and current in accordance with prescribed radiation safety procedures.
g.Practice radiation protection techniques by incorporating beam restrictive devices, patient shielding and selecting proper exposure factors to minimize radiation exposure to patients.
h.Monitor accessory equipment and assist physician, as required.
i.Record amount and type of film exposed and label film with identifying information.
j.Process film in accordance with standard procedures.
k.Calibrate, clean and perform routine maintenance on equipment to ensure proper functioning.
l.Troubleshoot problems with imaging equipment, as required.
m.Clean and sterilize instruments, supplies and equipment, stock and replenish supplies as required.
n.Maintain work area in clean and orderly condition.

2.Provision of clerical support to the Radiology Department. [10%]

a.Maintain accurate patient exam histories on both the appropriate jacket and on daily log sheets.*
b.Assist with annual department purge of non-current films.*
c.Back up both reception and file areas including filing, patient scheduling and answering phones.*

3.Responsible for providing team support to Radiology Department. [4%]

a.Research, develop and communicate new ideas, trends and techniques.
b.Maintain currency of CE units.
c.Provide support to other Radiologic Technologists including technical and clerical assistance to encourage smooth patient flow.
d.Participate in departmental staff meetings and TQM project teams.

4. Performance of other duties as assigned by the departmental manager or team leader. [2%]

*Non-essential functions if in a team setting. Essential work duties if in an independent radiologic technologist position.
