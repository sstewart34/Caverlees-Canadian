About the Job


ABOUT CATALINA: The world is changing, consumers are changing and so is Catalina- we are becoming a consumer-centric, multi-channel, loyalty company, committed to helping manufacturer and retail brands deliver unprecedented performance by unleashing the power to know, engage and empower the worlds consumers. With integrated in and out-of-store marketing platforms, Catalina pioneers breakthrough consumer loyalty solutions that transform the shopping experience. We have the unique ability to listen, understand and respond to consumers over time, and we are creating ways to connect with her whenever, wherever, and however she chooses on her path to purchase.


Catalina leverages the world's largest, transaction-level, shopper-data warehouse to develop, deliver, and measure consumer and patient-driven engagements. With average promotional response rates of 6.8% - one of the highest in the industry, only Catalina knows the evolving buying decisions and individual needs of shoppers. Catalina uses this understanding to create customized, measurable campaigns that motivate high-value consumers to try new products, increase consumption and stay loyal to brands. Catalina Health adopts similar methodologies working to improve behaviors one health consumer at a timewith highly relevant and personalized messages.


At Catalina, we have the privilege of helping people make the best choices for themselves and their families. This purpose is at the heart of Catalinas transformational change. Catalina is based in St. Petersburg, Florida, with operations in the US, Europe and Japan. To learn more, please visit www.CatalinaMarketing.com


Job Summary:

Catalina operates platforms that are large, complex, geographically dispersed combinations of IT staff, third party service providers, automation, networking, telephony, storage, computing, data warehouse, monitoring and related user administration and support services.


The Sr. Director, Infrastructure Architecture & Strategy is responsible for leading the Infrastructure Architecture & Strategy team that ensures Catalina designs, plans, architectures, processes, etc., retain compatibility with enterprise and solution architectures, and conform to appropriate standards (corporate, industry, national and international) within constraints of cost, security and sustainability. Core responsibilities aremanaging infrastructure architecture, infrastructure technology innovation,technology roadmaps, technology strategy, and infrastructure standards management. The range of technologies includes networks/connectivity, enterprise computing, storage, database technology and personal/mobile computing. In addition, this role is responsible to provide technical expertise for the existing services portfolio. The team supports/collaborates with the Design & Delivery, Service & Support, Information Security, and Enterprise Architecture teams in creating a customer excellence culture that promotes a secure computing environment.


The Sr. Director, Infrastructure Architecture & Strategy partners with Product and Platform Managers to generate new enterprise-wide strategies for improving effectiveness to match changing business and technology requirements. Build and sustain collaborative relationships and alliances across internal, external and organizational boundaries. Conduct best practices and benchmarking studies in order to recommend optimal solutions.


This position is responsible for the creation and roll-out of infrastructure and Operations (I&O) architecture standards,roadmap, infrastructure strategy, promote infrastructure innovation, and provide direction and guidance to staff, management and all related stakeholders on all aspects of the development of, and modifications to, Infrastructure architecture and standards.


Essential Functions and Responsibilities:

Ability to critically evaluate information, anticipate and understand the implications of decisions, and develop and execute action plans based on overall business strategy

Knowledge of the enterprise systems and architecture standards and how they apply to business functions/process, as-well- as the implications regarding hardware and software

Ability to develop technology and investment priorities based on business needs

Experience with developing and maintaining enterprise infrastructure architecture plans and associated technology roadmaps

Lead the Infrastructure strategy development and creation of an infrastructure roadmap that will support enterprise wide deployment of new technology based products and services

Participate in the formulation of the IT Strategy and Business Plan as a member of the IT Leadership Team.

Significant multi-technology experience and understanding of applications development as well as infrastructure management

Perform all functions related to staff management including: mentoring and coaching for high performance; performance planning, development and monitoring including mid-year and annual reviews; recruiting and termination.

Lead staff in the adherence to the IT Operating Model best practices including all aspects of program and project life cycle management.

Prepare timely status reporting (daily issues, weekly status, etc.) with accurate, appropriate information.

Develop and monitor the annual Capital and Expense plans and budgets.

Monitor activities and processes to ensure the quality of technical and business solutions.

PRINCIPAL CHALLENGES:

Most typical - Recommending to VPs and SVPs in other parts of Catalina that they operate within, and adhere to, specific constraints regarding technology roadmaps, technology strategy, and infrastructure standards management. A typical challenge that the incumbent faces will include influencing others without direct authority while defining and overseeing design plans and architectures. The successful adoption and implementation of these plans and conformance to these architectures is critical to the success of ability to deliver and support infrastructure platforms that support application software and services to stores, customers, .com and all IT-supported services. The incumbent must lead collaboration and negotiation with his/ her peers on other to ensure development of technology roadmaps, technology strategy, and infrastructure standards management. The incumbent must communicate effectively with IT, business and partner/vendorexecutives, sell the values of technology roadmaps, technology strategies, and infrastructure standards, and serve as an advocate for maturation within the architecture and strategy space.


Most complex - Synthesizing and deciding upon correct courses of action given conflicting input regarding technology roadmaps, technology strategies, and infrastructure standards expectations from various sources across Catalina. The incumbent requires solid understanding and experience with cross-functional Technology processes for infrastructure and service strategies, development, delivery and operations in order to direct, monitor and govern architectures and plans for same. The incumbent must be capable of varying his/ her communications style by audience to work effectively with all levels of IT professionals including project managers, engineers, functional managers, Directors, Vice Presidents, Sr. Vice Presidents, vendors and business leaders to understand their work processes and leverage IT best practices to develop appropriate I&O architectural plans and strategies.


Short term - Rapidly developing a sufficient understanding of Catalina environment and capability in order to staff manager roles with the correct skill sets. Quickly building trusting and valued relationships with key resources from functions across I&O and IT.


Long term - Making recommendations to evolve the function and operation of Catalina Infrastructure.


AUTHORITY AND RESPONSIBILITY:

The incumbent will have the authority to make decisions on resources and spend within the pre-determined budget for the program. The incumbent will determine the budget and resources needed to be successful and propose for senior management or executives for approval. The incumbent will direct development of plans, policies, agreements and commitments for architectural guidelines, strategies, and innovation for I&O, and participate with other Catalina architectural groups, bodies and functions, including the Enterprise Architecture team. The incumbent will also have the responsibility to hire the resources he/ she needs (external or internal) and will have accountability to take corrective action for resources that are not performing. The incumbent will manage performance for direct reports including salary increases and have input into the performance evaluations for indirect reports. The incumbent will have the authority to determine methodology to be used for architecture and strategy for I&O, and the platforms and services I&O provides. The incumbent will also direct and agree criteria for success of I&O design and delivery initiatives including how to measure adherence to architectures, and methods and formats (such asbalanced scorecard, dashboards) for presenting performance and highlightingactions required.
